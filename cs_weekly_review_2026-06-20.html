📋 Executive Summary

This was a lower-volume week, with overall ticket count down 15.9% week-over-week (760 vs 904). The decline is almost entirely Collections (−157, 502 vs 659), and roughly half of that drop is noise/spam tickets (−76). On the Support side the channels were stable to up: CrediPay widget +25.5% (59 vs 47), WhatsApp Support +3.4% (154 vs 149), and CrediPay email −8.2% (45 vs 49).

On Customer Support performance (calculated only over the 258 Support tickets — WhatsApp Support, CrediPay Support widget and CrediPay email — excluding Collections), CSAT recovered to 95.6% (+5.9 pp from 89.7%), back above the 95% target. SLA held at 96.4% (+0.5 pp from 95.9%), comfortably above the 90% target, with average net business minutes dropping from 40.0 to 28.2 minutes. Deflection fell to 48.6% (−5.7 pp from 54.3%), still below the 70% target and worth watching. CSAT response rate was 17.4% (45 answers from 258 surveys), up from 15.9%.

On Collections, CSAT held at 94.4% (17 of 18 answered positive, flat vs the prior week), with response rate easing up to 3.6% (18 answers from 502 surveys) from 2.7%.

Three detractors this week (vs 4 last week), all rated 1: two on the CrediPay Support widget (both sellers, both flagged human-required — one financial-reconciliation, one credit-limit-check) and one on WhatsApp Collections (a buyer, on a payment-proof case). The two Support detractors are the entire reason CSAT did not score even higher — on a base of only 45 answered Support surveys, two negatives carry weight. CrediPay email continues with no survey engagement: 0 responses from 45 surveys sent.
