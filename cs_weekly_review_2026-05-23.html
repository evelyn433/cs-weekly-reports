📋 Executive Summary

This was an intense week with several operational inconsistencies that ended up impacting our KPIs across the board. On top of that, Thursday May 21 was AI Day at Credix, which meant the Customer Support operation was running only partially throughout the day. The combination of these factors helps explain the simultaneous drop in CSAT, SLA and Deflection — more details in Operations Insights below.

Total ticket volume for the week was 1,081 tickets, down slightly from 1,127 the previous week (−46, −4%). Volume distribution shifted: WhatsApp Support grew 10% (143 vs 130), CrediPay email grew 19% (63 vs 53), CrediPay widget stayed flat (65), and WhatsApp Collections dropped 8% (810 vs 879).

On Customer Support performance (calculated only over the 271 Support tickets — WhatsApp Support, CrediPay Support widget and CrediPay email — excluding Collections), all three headline KPIs deteriorated this week. CSAT fell to 89.3%, below the 95% target and down 8.3 pp from 97.6%. SLA dropped to 84.1%, below the 90% target and down 9.8 pp from 93.9%, with average resolution time (net business minutes) tripling from 22.2 to 62.3 minutes. Deflection fell to 29.1% (vs 40.7%, −11.6 pp), well below the 70% target. CSAT response rate dropped to 10.4% (28 answers) vs 16.5%.

On Collections, CSAT was essentially flat at 82.6% (vs 83.3%, −0.7 pp), but the response rate halved from 7.4% to 3.9% (23 answers from 583 surveys sent).

Eight detractors this week (vs 8 last week), but the pattern shifted: 5 in WhatsApp Collections, 2 in WhatsApp Support, 1 in CrediPay widget. One Support detractor (#31096) appears to be a misclick — rating 1 with the comment "Achei excelente atendimento, mas não consigo dar mais estrelinhas, não está permitindo" — and the CrediPay widget detractor (#31779) is the only one with a substantive complaint ("Não houve retorno, após longa espera"). The remaining detractors are mostly in Collections without comments. CrediPay email CSAT instrumentation continues to lag — only 1 response from 62 surveys sent this week (vs 2 the previous week).

🔧 Operations Insights & Follow-ups

This week had several boleto-related incidents that materially impacted the Support KPIs. Combined with AI Day on Thursday (when the operation ran only partially), these issues explain the simultaneous drop in CSAT, SLA and Deflection.

1. Boleto download failing with 404 / PIE003 · SUP-61

Boleto download was returning 404 PIE003 "Bankslip not found" for an existing repayment flow. The failure happened both via the email link sent to buyers and from Ops itself, affecting buyers' ability to access their boletos. Thread.

Root cause: BTG BaaS confirmed that BolePix issuance was being performed reusing the same clientRequestId previously used to generate a Pix QR code (or two BolePix issuances within ~150 ms of each other). Since clientRequestId is a strong idempotency key, this caused BTG to cancel the issued BolePix transactions. Resolution: hotfixed by Roney/Luis with manual BolePix recreation for affected cases. A separate ticket SUP-77 was opened for the root-cause fix.

2. Praso · paid boletos still showing as open · SUP-62

Multiple Pix payments made over the weekend were leaving the related boletos marked as "open" in our system. Praso reported 3 concrete examples across 2 CNPJs (64857809000117 and 41692797000112). Thread.

Same symptom as SUP-39 from the previous week — this is now becoming a recurring pattern with Praso. Jin (capital team) verified that the boletos were indeed "Liquidado" on BTG's side, but the status was not propagating to our Collections view. Roney is working in parallel on SUP-64 (Praso reporting errors on the repayments webhook), which likely shares a root cause.

3. Boleto generation failing — no link available for download · SUP-63

For several orders, the boleto link was simply not being generated, so buyers had no way to access or pay them. Multiple cases were reported throughout the week across different CNPJs. Thread.

Linked to the recent cluster of boleto-link generation issues (PE-2692) and likely sharing the same BTG BolePix cancellation root cause as SUP-61. Engineering team went into warroom mode with BTG to investigate. Side effect: some cases needed renegotiation because their due dates had already passed when the boleto was finally recovered, which generates additional friction with buyers and sellers.

4. CrediPay email CSAT instrumentation · still stagnant

Third week of monitoring and engagement remains very low. This week the CrediPay (email) channel produced only 1 response from 62 surveys sent (1.6% response rate, positive). Although volume in this inbox grew (53 → 63 tickets), the survey response rate has not picked up. May be worth investigating whether the survey link is reaching the inbox correctly, if emails are being filtered, or if the email format itself is not generating engagement.

🔍 Structural recommendations — recurring Praso friction

Beyond this week's specific incidents, three patterns have appeared repeatedly with Praso. Worth evaluating viability of structural fixes:

5. Automated boleto write-off after payment confirmation

Cases where payment was made but boletos remained "open" are becoming frequent (SUP-39 last week, SUP-62 this week). Recommendation: investigate the feasibility of automatically writing off boletos as soon as the payment is confirmed by BTG / SEC, instead of relying on manual reconciliation. This would prevent buyer impact and reduce friction with Praso, since today the partner has to flag each case individually before we can resolve it.

6. Automatic re-enablement of CNPJ eligibility after settlement

Today, when a buyer's CNPJ becomes ineligible due to an overdue boleto, the CNPJ does not automatically become eligible again after the boleto is paid or after Praso cancels the order. This forces Praso to contact us specifically to re-enable each CNPJ, generating ongoing back-and-forth. Recommendation: investigate the feasibility of triggering automatic CNPJ re-eligibility when the underlying boleto is settled or the order is cancelled.

7. Better visibility into payments received vs boletos paid

We had recent cases of duplicate payments on the same boleto with Praso. Initial Finops review reported that only one payment had been received, but further investigation revealed that the duplicate amount had in fact been received. By the time this was confirmed, significant friction had already built up between Credix, Praso, and the buyer. Recommendation: study the possibility of a clearer view (Ops dashboard or report) that tracks payments received against boletos paid, making it easy to trace and reconcile payments at the boleto level. This would significantly speed up our response to Praso when these cases arise and reduce friction during investigation.
