📋 Executive Summary

Total ticket volume tripled to 1,127 tickets, up from 369 the previous week (+758, +205%). The spike is concentrated entirely in WhatsApp Collections (129 → 879, +581%), with l2_noise_or_spam jumping from 8 to 274 tickets. This looks like an automation/campaign pulse on the Collections side rather than organic demand. Support volume (CrediPay email + WhatsApp Support + CrediPay widget) was essentially flat at 248 tickets (vs 240).

On Customer Support performance (calculated only over the 248 Support tickets — WhatsApp Support, CrediPay Support widget and CrediPay email — excluding Collections), CSAT reached 97.5%, above the 95% target and slightly up from 97.2%. SLA held at 93.1%, above the 90% target and up from 92.5%, with average net business minutes halving from 53.7 to 26.6 min. Deflection rose to 40.7% (+4.9pp) but remains well below the 70% target. CSAT response rate improved to 16.1% (40 answers vs 36).

On Collections, CSAT slipped to 83.3% (vs 91.7%, −8.3pp) and response rate dropped to 4.8% (vs 9.3%) — although the absolute base tripled (42 vs 12 answers). The bigger denominator combined with frustration from over-messaging pulled the score down.

8 detractors this week (7×1-rating, 1×2-rating); 7 of 8 came from WhatsApp Collections, concentrated in Praso (4), Allied (2) and Fujioka (1) buyers, all on l2_boleto_requested / l2_payment_proof_sent. One Praso buyer comment explicitly complains about over-messaging on pre-due invoices ("Diversas mensagens lembrando uma fatura que nem venceu"). The single Support detractor (#29643, Nors) has comment "excelente" — likely a rating misclick.

The Collections volume + noise/spam surge is the headline operational signal — worth investigating whether a reminder cadence change, automation regression, or new outbound campaign triggered it. Details in Operations Insights below.

🔧 Operations Insights & Follow-ups

1. CrediPay email CSAT instrumentation — first responses received

After several weeks of monitoring with no responses, the CrediPay (email) channel produced its first 2 CSAT responses this week, both rated 4–5 (good). Response rate is still very low (3.77% on 53 surveys sent), but the end-to-end flow is confirmed working. We'll continue monitoring to see whether response rate climbs as buyers get used to the email prompt, or whether we need to revisit the email copy/CTA.

2. Collections volume + noise/spam surge — automation pulse suspected

WhatsApp Collections volume jumped 7× WoW (129 → 879) and tickets tagged l2_noise_or_spam in Collections went from 8 to 274 (+3,325%). Praso buyers alone account for 499 of the 1,127 weekly tickets (44%), with single-day peaks of 166 and 131. Two Praso detractor comments explicitly complain about over-messaging on invoices that aren't yet due ("Diversas mensagens lembrando uma fatura que nem venceu"; "Não sou a favor de estar recebendo esse tipo de mensagem eu sei os meus compromissos").

This pattern — high volume + high noise/spam + buyer complaints about reminder cadence — strongly suggests an automation, reminder cadence, or outbound campaign change on the Collections side, particularly affecting Praso. Action needed: confirm whether anything changed in the Collections reminder logic, Aira Collections flow, or outbound messaging for Praso this week, and whether the cadence can be tuned down. If no change was made, the spam tag classifier may be over-firing.

3. Boleto lookup flow may be broken

Detractor #29908 (Praso buyer, May 13) reports: "A consulta de boletos não funciona" (the boleto lookup is not working). Given the Praso-heavy detractor pattern and that l2_boleto_requested is the dominant L2 tag on both detractors and Support tickets (60 of 248), this single complaint may signal a broader Praso-specific issue with the boleto self-service flow. Action needed: reproduce the boleto lookup for a Praso buyer to confirm whether the flow is functional, and check Aira's boleto handler for recent changes.

4. Support performance held strong despite Collections noise

Despite the massive Collections volume spike, Support performance improved across the board: CSAT 97.5% (+0.3pp), SLA 93.1% (+0.6pp), Deflection 40.7% (+4.9pp), avg net business minutes for resolution dropped from 53.7 to 26.6 (-50%). Worth highlighting that the Aira + human team kept Support quality intact while Collections was generating 7× more tickets — good signal on operational isolation between the two queues.
