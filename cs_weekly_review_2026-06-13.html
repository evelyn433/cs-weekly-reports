📋 Executive Summary

This was a higher-volume week, with overall ticket count up 11.9% week-over-week (904 vs 808). Growth was spread across every channel, led by CrediPay email (+96%, 49 vs 25) and WhatsApp Support (+32%, 149 vs 113). The CrediPay widget rose +18% (47 vs 40) and Collections grew +5% (659 vs 630).

On Customer Support performance (calculated only over the 245 Support tickets — WhatsApp Support, CrediPay Support widget and CrediPay email — excluding Collections), CSAT fell to 90.6% (−6.4 pp from 97.0%), landing below the 95% target. The drop is driven by 3 detractors on WhatsApp Support out of only 16 answered surveys, so the small denominator amplifies each negative rating. On the operational side the picture is strong: SLA rose to 97.0% (+4.7 pp from 92.3%), well above the 90% target, with average net business minutes dropping from 99.4 to 27.4 minutes. Deflection eased to 53.1% (−2.5 pp from 55.6%), still below the 70% target. CSAT response rate was 13.3% (32 answers from 241 surveys), down from 18.6%.

On Collections, CSAT improved to 94.4% (+10.2 pp from 84.2%, 17 of 18 answered positive), with response rate easing to 3.8% (18 answers from 480 surveys) from 4.6%.

Four detractors this week (vs 4 last week): three on WhatsApp Support (ratings 1, 2 and 3) and one on WhatsApp Collections (rating 2). All four carry the l2_boleto_requested tag. One rating-3 ticket (#34040) is shown as neutral in the Chatwoot UI but still counts against CSAT, because the metric is calculated as good ÷ answered. The concentration on boleto requests suggests auditing that flow — particularly weekend/after-hours handling and renegotiation cases — is the highest-leverage CSAT action this week. CrediPay email continues with no survey engagement: 0 responses from 45 surveys sent, despite volume nearly doubling.

Two operational items stand out: a critical WhatsApp send failure on Friday 12/06 (SUP-214) caused by broad Meta API instability that also affected Instagram — some outbound messages may have been lost — and a structural timezone bug on disbursement timestamps (SUP-191). Both are covered in Operations Insights below.

🔧 Operations Insights & Follow-ups

Items 1 and 2 are improvement recommendations carried over from previous weeks (both still open). Items 3 onward are tickets and issues raised in the #general-support channel during the week of 07/06 to 13/06, ordered chronologically.

1. Validate contact unlink functionality (phone / email per CNPJ)

It would be valuable to verify whether the functionality to unlink a phone number or email from a given CNPJ is working correctly. We have indications that numbers we had marked to be unlinked are still receiving messages. Additionally, we should validate whether new contacts being registered are being properly saved and used by the system for outbound communications.

2. Adjust Aira behavior outside business hours and on holidays

It would be helpful to adjust the messages Aira sends on weekends and Friday from 18h onward. When Aira needs to escalate to human support during these periods, it would be better if she instructed the customer to reach out again on Monday from 9h. There is no operation on weekends, and depending on the timestamp of ticket creation, if more than 24 hours have elapsed we are no longer able to respond by Monday because the response window has closed.

Also, on holidays or any events where there is no operation, Aira should ideally send an informational message setting the right expectation about when the customer will be attended.

13. SUP-191 · Disbursement timestamp discrepancy — timezone bug (08/06)

Nors/Volvo reported a R$ 290,42 difference between the disbursement reported (R$ 170.496,29) and the amount received (R$ 170.205,87) on 2026-06-03. Root cause (Jin, 10/06): BTG sends only the date of the disbursement without a time; we store it as midnight, and on the UTC→BRT conversion (−3h) the timestamp shifts to 21h of the previous day, mis-bucketing the entry. This is a structural timezone bug, not a one-off — worth fixing at the storage/conversion layer so disbursement dates reconcile correctly going forward.

14. SUP-214 · WhatsApp messages failing to send — critical incident (12/06)

Investigation pointed to broad Meta API instability that day, which also affected Instagram (press coverage). The issue cleared by end of day, but some outbound messages may have been lost in the window. Example conversation: 34778.
