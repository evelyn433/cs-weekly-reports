📋 Executive Summary

A difficult week on Support quality, on the highest volume we have measured. Total tickets crossed 1,001 for the first time (+17.4% vs 853), with growth across every channel: CrediPay email +56% (64 vs 41), WhatsApp Support +8.5% (179 vs 165), the widget +26% (48 vs 38), and WhatsApp Collections +16.6% (710 vs 609). The volume surge is concentrated from July 1 onward (Wednesday July 1 peaked at 274 tickets), right at the start of Q3.

On Customer Support performance (over the 291 Support tickets, excluding Collections), every KPI missed its target. CSAT fell to 91.3% (−3.7 pp, below the 95% target). SLA dropped to 85.4% (−9.7 pp) — the first time it has fallen below the 90% target in this weekly series — and average net business minutes tripled to 87.3 minutes (from 28.2). Deflection fell to 41.6% (−12.5 pp), and CSAT response rate roughly halved to 7.9% (23 answers from 291 surveys), so there is less signal behind the CSAT reading.

On Collections, CSAT was 85.7% (−3.2 pp, 6 of 7 positive) with response rate collapsing to 1.0% (just 7 answers from 710 surveys) — the thinnest response we have recorded.

Three detractors this week (2 Support, 1 Collections), rated 1 and 3, all buyers, all boleto-related. One (#37016) left a positive comment ("Muito satisfeito") yet scored 3 — a likely mis-tap; excluding it would lift Support CSAT to 95.2%, back at target. CrediPay email returned to 0 survey responses despite its 56% volume jump.

Channel observations

CrediPay Support (widget) remained the healthiest channel — 25.0% response rate (12 of 48), all 12 positive — and its volume recovered a little (38 → 48).
