📋 Executive Summary

A strong week on Support quality, on higher overall volume. Total tickets rose 12.2% week-over-week (853 vs 760), driven almost entirely by Collections (+107, 609 vs 502), of which roughly a third of the increase was noise/spam. Support channels were mixed: WhatsApp Support +7% (165 vs 154), while the CrediPay widget fell (38 vs 59) and email eased (41 vs 45).

On Customer Support performance (over the 244 Support tickets — WhatsApp Support, CrediPay widget and CrediPay email, excluding Collections), CSAT rose to 97.1% (+1.4 pp from 95.7%), comfortably above the 95% target. SLA held at 96.6% (+0.5 pp from 96.1%), well above the 90% target, with average net business minutes dropping to 19.9 minutes (from 32.7) — the fastest reading recorded. Deflection recovered to 54.1% (+5.6 pp from 48.5%), still below the 70% target. CSAT response rate was 13.9% (34 answers from 244 surveys), down from 18.2%.

On Collections, CSAT slipped to 88.9% (−5.6 pp from 94.4%, 16 of 18 answered positive), with response rate easing to 3.0% (18 answers from 609 surveys).

Three detractors this week (same as last week), all rated 1: one on WhatsApp Support and two on WhatsApp Collections — all buyers, all boleto-related. One of them (#35834) left a clearly positive comment ("Nota 10. Atendido na hora") yet scored 1, a likely mis-tap; excluding it would put Support CSAT at 100%. CrediPay email finally captured 2 survey responses this week (vs 0 the prior weeks).

Channel observations

CrediPay Support (widget) is still the strongest response channel — 34.2% response rate (13 of 38), all 13 positive — but its volume keeps shrinking (59 → 38), so it captured fewer absolute responses than prior weeks.

WhatsApp Support returned 94.7% CSAT (18 of 19 positive) at an 11.5% response rate; the single detractor (#35834) carries a positive comment and looks like a mis-tap.

CrediPay (email) captured 2 responses this week (both positive) — the first survey engagement on this channel after weeks at zero. Thin, but worth watching whether it holds.

WhatsApp Collections CSAT slipped to 88.9% (16 of 18 positive, 2 detractors) at a 3.0% response rate. Spam-tagged tickets were ~31% of Collections volume (186 of 609).
