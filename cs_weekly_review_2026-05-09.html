📋 Executive Summary

Total ticket volume for the week dropped to 368 tickets, down from 1,136 the previous week. This week we did not see a high number of spam tickets nor boleto inquiries (l2_noise_or_spam fell from 251 to 20, and Collections volume dropped from 904 to 128), most likely as a side effect of the Meta payment issue described in the Operations Insights section below, which prevented WhatsApp messages from being delivered during the week. WhatsApp Support grew 16.8% (139 vs 119), the only channel trending up.

On Customer Support performance (calculated only over the 240 Support tickets — WhatsApp Support, CrediPay Support widget and CrediPay email — excluding Collections), CSAT reached 97.14%, above the 95% target and up from 95.24% last week. SLA recovered to 92.61%, above the 90% target and well above last week's 76.52%, with average resolution time (net business minutes) dropping from 125.6 to 43.3 minutes. Deflection doubled to 35.98% (vs 21.12%), but remains well below the 70% target. CSAT response rate dropped to 14.58% (35 answers) vs 18.10%.

On Collections, CSAT rose to 91.67% (vs 87.10%) and response rate nearly tripled to 9.38% (12 answers), a direct effect of the lower volume this week.

Only 2 detractors this week, both bot-resolved without human escalation. We suggest reviewing the flows for l2_credit_limit_increase and l2_boleto_requested for cases where the automated answer does not fully resolve the request.

Two operational incidents this week: (i) Meta WhatsApp Business payment issue that disrupted message delivery (now resolved by Stijn), and (ii) Praso boletos processed via Stone/BTG with refund and "open status" issues impacting R$ 7,315 across multiple buyers — all cases were renegotiated by EOD Friday May 08. Both detailed in Operations Insights below.

🔧 Operations Insights & Follow-ups

1. CrediPay email CSAT instrumentation

Chatwoot has been updated and the survey response link is already being sent via email, but we have not received any responses yet. We will keep monitoring over the next weeks to assess channel performance.

2. Deflection drop driven by Meta payment issue

The deflection drop relative to QTD is explained primarily by lower overall ticket volume, particularly in the categories Aira is best at deflecting. The root cause, as flagged in the thread, was a payment issue on the Meta (WhatsApp Business) account, which caused message delivery failures and massively reduced collections reminder delivery throughout the week. Payment has since been settled by Stijn. Next step: set up direct monitoring of the Meta vendor (usage and billing) to prevent recurrence, as no alerts were received for failed top-ups or unpaid invoices.

3. Praso boletos · Stone/BTG payment processing issue

On Monday May 05, multiple Praso buyers reported boletos paid on May 04 that were either refunded or remained in "open" status despite payment confirmation. All cases involved payments processed through Stone Instituição de Pagamento S.A. Total impact: R$ 7,315, concentrated enough to rule out coincidence. Full context in the thread.

Resolution: Given the recent friction with Praso, Enzo approved an exception to renegotiate all affected boletos for payment on Friday May 08, conditional on Praso forwarding renegotiated boletos directly to buyers. All cases were renegotiated by EOD Friday.
