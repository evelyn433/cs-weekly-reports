📋 Executive Summary

Total ticket volume for the week jumped to 1,127 tickets, up from 370 the previous week (+757, roughly 3x). The increase was almost entirely driven by the WhatsApp Collections channel, which went from 129 to 879 tickets (+750), of which 274 were tagged as l2_noise_or_spam — a 34x increase from the 8 spam tickets the prior week. Support channels stayed relatively stable: WhatsApp Support −9, CrediPay widget +4, CrediPay email +12.

On Customer Support performance (calculated only over the 248 Support tickets — WhatsApp Support, CrediPay Support widget and CrediPay email — excluding Collections), CSAT held at 97.4%, well above the 95% target and essentially flat WoW. SLA at 94.6%, also above the 90% target and flat WoW, with average resolution time (net business minutes) improving from 28.1 to 21.8 minutes. Deflection rose to 40.3% (vs 35.3%, +5.0 pp), continuing the upward trend but still well below the 70% target. CSAT response rate flat at 15.3% (38 answers) vs 15.4%.

On Collections, the picture is different: CSAT dropped sharply to 82.9% (vs 91.7%, −8.8 pp), with response rate falling to 4.7% (41 answers from 879 surveys). The drop is real and now statistically meaningful — the denominator went from 12 to 41 answered surveys, and 7 of the 8 detractors this week landed in Collections.

Eight detractors this week (vs 2 the previous week), with a clear theme: buyers reacting negatively to the cadence and timing of Collections WhatsApp messages (e.g., "Diversas mensagens lembrando uma fatura que nem venceu"). One Support detractor (#29643) appears to be a misclick — rating 1 with a positive comment "excelente". The Praso/Stone and Meta WhatsApp incidents from last week were both resolved with no new occurrences.

🔧 Operations Insights & Follow-ups

1. CrediPay email CSAT instrumentation · monitoring

The Chatwoot email survey update generated 2 responses this week (vs 0 last week), both positive. Response rate is still very low at 3.8%, but the channel is now producing data. Will continue monitoring to see if engagement grows in the coming weeks.

2. Sharp spike in Collections volume and spam-tagged tickets

WhatsApp Collections went from 129 to 879 tickets (+750), and l2_noise_or_spam tickets jumped from 8 to 274 — roughly 31% of this week's Collections volume is being tagged as noise/spam. The cause is the normalization of the Meta WhatsApp Business payment: once the payment was settled, the payment reminder and collection messages started being delivered again, generating the inbound spike (including the spam tickets that come naturally with the higher volume of outbound messages).

3. Recurring issues with Praso — payments not reflecting in Ops

We are seeing some kind of Praso-specific issue on a weekly basis. This week, the problem was payments that had been completed by buyers but were still showing as "open" / overdue in our system. Ticket SUP-39 was opened to investigate. Initial findings: 4 payments across 2 CNPJs (38439298000103 and 31251618000158, R$ 1.116,40 + R$ 776,35 via PIX on 11/05) had payment receipts attached but the related boletos were still showing as open. One root cause confirmed by Jin: one of the buyers paid using the Credix PIX key directly instead of the bolepix, which breaks the automatic conciliation — these cases require manual workarounds from our side. Another open case still under investigation: CNPJ 41692797000112 / NF 872165 — boleto paid via PIX on 16/05, status shows "Liquidado" on BTG but still "Open" in our Collections view (Asset ID 2b354097-885e-4d9b-a581-0a8f13790060). Full thread here. Next step: data eng to investigate the reconciliation gap between BTG status and Collections status, and we should give Praso buyers clearer instructions to always use the bolepix.
